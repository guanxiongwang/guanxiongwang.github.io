I am an incoming Ph.D. student in XXX at XXX.

My research interests lie at the intersection of environmental psychology and the global climate challenge. Broadly speaking, my research aims to unravel three questions: 1) How do individuals across different societies perceive, evaluate, and respond to global climate change? 2) What are the culture-specific enablers and barriers to high-impact pro-climate actions? 3) How can this understanding facilitate the development of more effective cross-cultural interventions to promote pro-climate behavior? My goal is to wield the power of psychology to develop practical, applied solutions to climate change through empirically grounded understandings of the people-environment link across cultures.

Before joining XXX, I got my bachelor's and master's degree in 2019 and 2021, respectively, from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign and the University of Pennsylvania where I studied Natural Resources and Environmental Sciences and Environmental Sustainability. After graduation, I worked as an Advisory associate for a year and more at PricewaterhouseCoopers (PwC) China to deliver business solutions to clients in the food industry.

In my free time, I enjoy petting and making observations about cat, taking photos of natural scences (primarily), working out, and visiting art museums.
